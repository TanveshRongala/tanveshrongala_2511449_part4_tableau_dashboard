# Dashboard Story for Leadership

## Executive Summary

The executive dashboard shows a business with **₹217.02M in sales**, **₹33.31M in profit**, and an overall **15.3% profit margin** across **4,200 orders**. Performance is not uniform: the strongest opportunities come from scaling high-profit categories and regions, while the main risks are margin dilution from discounts, slower shipping modes, and return concentration in specific categories or regions.

## What Is Performing Well

- **South** is the strongest sales region with **₹64.69M** in sales and **₹9.99M** in profit.
- **Technology** is the most profitable category with **₹28.04M** in profit.
- **Copiers** is the highest-profit sub-category with **₹7.31M** profit and **18.0%** margin.
- **Home Office** is the leading customer segment by sales at **₹74.50M**.

## What Is Underperforming

- **West** has the weakest regional profit margin at **15.1%**.
- **Office Supplies** is the weakest category by total profit at **₹1.71M**.
- **Paper** is the largest sub-category profit drag at **₹0.32M**.
- The **>25%** discount band has the weakest margin at **6.5%**, indicating possible discount leakage.

## Visible Risks

The dashboard highlights three primary risks. First, discounts may be driving sales volume but weakening profit quality. Second, return patterns are concentrated: **Furniture** has the highest category return rate at **7.7%**, while **East** has the highest regional return rate at **4.9%**. Third, shipping delays are not equal across modes; **Standard Class** averages **4.7 delivery days**, which could affect ratings, repeat purchase behavior, and returns.

## Visible Opportunities

The strongest opportunity is to scale areas that combine high revenue and strong margin. Leadership should protect inventory and service levels for profitable sub-categories such as **Copiers**, and investigate why lower-performing categories/sub-categories lose margin. There is also an opportunity to refine discount strategy by replacing broad discounting with targeted offers based on segment, category, and return risk.

## Recommended Business Actions

1. Tighten discount approval rules for weak-margin discount bands and products.
2. Prioritize inventory availability for the most profitable sub-categories.
3. Launch a return-reduction deep dive for **Furniture** and **East**.
4. Review fulfillment SLAs for **Standard Class** and compare delivery days against customer ratings.
5. Use region and segment filters in the dashboard during business reviews to decide where action should be local versus enterprise-wide.

## Limitations of the Dashboard

The dashboard is based on available order-level fields only. It does not include marketing spend, inventory stockout history, competitor pricing, product cost breakdown, or customer lifetime value. Return flag is treated as binary per order row, and delivery days are assumed to be already calculated correctly from order and ship dates.

## Suggested Next Analysis

Next analysis should quantify discount incrementality, identify drivers of returns using category/product and shipping delay combinations, and compare profitability after incorporating campaign spend. Leadership may also want a customer cohort view to understand whether high-value segments are repeat purchasers or one-time buyers.
